How it works

Why Stake gets blocked and why mirrors are needed

In different countries, access to online platforms can be restricted by law or by local regulators. Stake mirrors are alternative domains that route you to the same platform but via a different address.

Reasons for blocking

Regional restrictions and filters

Access to the main Stake domain can be limited by providers via IP, DNS, or based on regulator decisions. As a result, the site doesn’t open even though the service itself continues to operate normally.

Role of mirrors

Alternative domains with the same content

A mirror is an address that leads to the same platform and the same account but uses a different domain. This helps you get access if the main domain is unavailable in a certain network or region.

Safe access

How to use Stake mirrors safely

It’s important that your access to the platform complies with the laws of your country, and that any actions you take are conscious and responsible. A few basic steps can help reduce risks.

1
Use only trusted links
Follow Stake mirrors only from sources you trust. Avoid random links from spam, comments, and suspicious email campaigns.
2
Check HTTPS and the domain
Before entering your login and password, make sure the connection is secure (padlock icon in the browser) and the domain doesn’t have extra letters, symbols, or strange subdomains.
3
Mind your device and network
Use an up-to-date browser, security software, and, if needed, a trusted VPN. Avoid logging in from shared or insecure devices.
4
Follow local laws and restrictions
Before using the platform, ensure that access to such services does not conflict with the legislation of your country. The responsibility for use lies with you as the user.
Reminder: keep track of the time and amounts you spend on such platforms. Use them responsibly and set your own limits in advance.

What is a working Stake mirror? +

Why might Stake not open? +

Yes, a Stake mirror normally gives access to the same account, balance, and features as the main domain. The key is to make sure you’re using an official alternative domain, not a phishing copy.
